CUSTODIAN - CATALYSTHACK 2019

In April 2019, I attended CatalystHack, an all female and non-binary hackathon to inspire the next generation of girls in STEM, sponsored by Rolls Royce. We were encouraged to focus our ingenuity to use tech to help solve a social issue - the STEM gender gap.

On The first day, I got to know some of the other women attending the event, and made up a group - Team 15!
We were tasked with creating a prototype and idea behind a game that could be used to encourage more young women to get involved with STEM subjects. We came up with 'Custodian', a game where the main character has to brings life back to a world devastated by climate change and deforestation, through nourishing the environment and taking care of the remaining animals.

After 24 hours working on our prototype, we presented our ideas to the judges and Custodian was given first prize!

It was my first hackathon experience, and it a lot of fun coming up with our idea and bringing it to life, with guidance from some lovely people at Bossa Studios.
